Personalizing Claude, Model Selector & Mobile

8–10 min · Settings · Models · Mobile App

👤
Custom InstructionsSettings → Profile. Tell Claude your role, school, student demographics, and preferred response style — once, forever.
🔧
What to Write in Custom InstructionsOne or two sentences covering your role, school context, students, and preferred response style.
Example · Custom Instructions
I am a 7th grade science teacher at a Title I middle school. My students are 30% English Language Learners. Keep responses practical and concise. Always include differentiation suggestions for ELL students and avoid jargon.
🧠
Model SelectorHaiku → quick tasks. Sonnet → everyday work. Opus → complex reasoning and long documents.
✅
Sonnet for This SeriesFor everything in this Knowledge Booster, Claude Sonnet is the right choice.
📱
Claude on MobileAvailable on iOS and Android. Download the Anthropic app, sign in with your same account.
🎙️
Voice Input on MobileTap the microphone on your keyboard to speak your prompts — great for on-the-go quick questions.

Power Habits — Working Smarter with Claude

11–13 min · Context · Workflow · Collaboration · Prompts you can use today

⚖️
Right-Size Your Model

Sonnet handles 95% of educator tasks. Use Haiku for quick rewrites and simple lookups. Reserve Opus for genuinely complex reasoning — it costs more of your usage quota.

📏
The Context Window

Claude re-reads your entire conversation every single time you send a message. Long threads get slow and start to drift. Keep conversations focused on one task.

🔁
Summarize & Continue

When you feel drag — responses getting less precise or slower — stop and use this prompt. Open a new chat, paste the summary, keep going at full speed.

Prompt · Quick Summary
Summarize where we started, what we've accomplished, and what still needs to be done. Keep it under 200 words.
⚡
The Deep Re-Prompt Move

The advanced version. Claude summarizes your progress AND generates the opening prompt for your next session. One step — zero lost context, ready to paste and go.

Prompt · Deep Summary + Re-Prompt
Summarize where we started this chat, where we currently are, and where we still need to go. Then turn that summary into a prompt I can use to start the next conversation and continue this work seamlessly.
📋
Session Summary — Do This Every Time

End every productive session with a Markdown summary. Drop it in at the start of your next session before asking anything else. Claude never loses the thread. Do this religiously, whether there are issues or not.

Prompt · Session Summary
Generate a Markdown session summary covering: what we set out to do, what we accomplished, key decisions made, and what comes next. I'll save this and drop it in at the start of our next session.
Prompt · Auto-Remind (add to Project Instructions)
At a natural stopping point in any session, remind me to generate a Markdown session summary before we close out.
📅
Julian Date Naming for Conversations

Name conversations with a Julian date + one or two word descriptor. The Julian date is the year + the day number of the year (e.g., day 67 of 2026 = 26067). Your project log becomes chronological and searchable.

Naming Pattern · Examples
26067-Unit-Overview 26071-Assessment-Draft 26085-Parent-Letter 26092-Rubric-Final
🗃️
One Task per Conversation

Create a Project, then tackle each task in its own focused thread. Lesson plan in one chat, assessment in another, family letter in a third. Organized, fast, and easy to revisit.

🤝
Set a Working Agreement

Open complex sessions by telling Claude your name, experience level, preferred pace, and how you want to collaborate. Claude takes these agreements seriously — every response that follows is better calibrated.

Prompt · Working Agreement (adapt to your context)
My name is [your name]. I want to [describe your goal]. I have [describe your experience level — e.g., "some experience with backward design but need a lot of help with the assessment piece"]. Show me step by step, but don't lay it all out at once. Go section by section. Lead and guide me — don't just generate the content. First discuss, align, and get my sign-off before we move forward. Be honest, direct, and supportive.
🎭
Assign Roles by Stage of Work

Each stage of a project needs a different lens. Assign Claude a role that matches what the work requires right now. Research assistant → writing partner → editor → evaluator. Quality compounds at every stage.

Prompt · Role Assignment (swap role to fit your stage)
For this session, you are acting as a [developmental editor / curriculum reviewer / student / acquisitions editor — choose one]. Your job is to [describe the specific lens — e.g., "evaluate the structure and argument of each section, not the sentence-level writing"]. Be direct about what isn't working.
