Phase 1
Surface learning
Build foundational knowledge and vocabulary. Students acquire essential facts and skills before deeper connections are possible.
SOLO level
Uni-structural → Multi-structural
Students identify single ideas, then collect multiple disconnected pieces of knowledge.
High-impact strategies
Spaced practice Revisit content across time intervals to strengthen retention
Retrieval practice Low-stakes quizzing and recall to consolidate memory
Direct instruction Explicit teacher-led explanation with modeling and feedback
Vocabulary programs Systematic word study tied to disciplinary concepts
Phase 2
Deep learning
Connect ideas and build conceptual understanding. Students move from knowing facts in isolation to seeing how they relate.
SOLO level
Relational
Students integrate multiple ideas into a coherent whole, understanding how parts relate to each other.
High-impact strategies
Jigsaw Collaborative expert groups teaching peers their piece of content
Reciprocal teaching Students alternate roles — summarizer, questioner, clarifier, predictor
Argumentation Structured debate using evidence to defend and challenge claims
Concept mapping Visual diagrams that make relationships between ideas explicit
Phase 3
Transfer learning
Apply knowledge to new, unfamiliar situations. Students demonstrate that understanding is flexible and generative, not just recalled.
SOLO level
Extended abstract
Students generalize beyond the taught content, creating new hypotheses, making connections across domains, and generating novel solutions.
High-impact strategies
Problem-solving teaching Novel, ill-structured problems that require flexible application of knowledge
Service learning Real-world community tasks that demand authentic application
Transfer strategies Deliberate prompting to connect learning across contexts and subjects
