Phase 1: Surface Learning

This phase focuses on acquiring foundational facts and individual skills. Students learn the "parts" before they can see the "whole."

SOLO Levels: Uni-structural & Multi-structural (Click to view)
Uni-structural: The student focuses on a single relevant aspect or fact (e.g., naming one planet).
Multi-structural: The student identifies several relevant aspects but sees them as independent pieces (e.g., listing all planets without explaining their orbits or differences).
Jigsaw Method d = 0.92
Definition: A cooperative learning strategy where students become "experts" in one subtopic and teach it to their peers.

Grade 3-6 Example: In a science unit on the Water Cycle, "Expert Group A" learns only about Evaporation, while Group B learns about Precipitation. They then return to their home groups to teach their specific term to their teammates.
Direct Instruction d = 0.56
Definition: Explicit teacher-led instruction that involves clear explanations, modeling, and guided practice.

Grade 3-6 Example: A 4th-grade teacher uses an "I Do, We Do, You Do" approach to teach long division, modeling the steps on the board before students try three problems with a partner.
Spaced Practice d = 0.62
Definition: Breaking learning into shorter sessions over a long period rather than one long "massed" session.

Grade 3-6 Example: Instead of a one-hour spelling marathon on Thursday, 3rd graders practice their spelling words for 10 minutes every morning for a week.
Retrieval Practice d = 0.49
Definition: Strengthening memory by forcing the brain to recall information without looking at notes.

Grade 3-6 Example: After reading a chapter on the Gold Rush, 5th graders close their books and write down three things they remember on a sticky note.
Phase 2: Deep Learning

This phase focuses on finding patterns and connecting ideas to build a deep conceptual understanding.

SOLO Level: Relational (Click to view)
Relational: The student integrates several parts into a coherent whole. They understand the relationship between different concepts (e.g., explaining how gravity affects planetary orbits).
Jigsaw Method (Deep) d = 0.92
Definition: Using peer-teaching to not just share facts, but to synthesize how sub-topics relate to one another.

Grade 3-6 Example: After the initial "fact-finding" jigsaw, students now work together to explain how Evaporation and Precipitation *work together* to sustain a local lake's water levels.
Argumentation d = 0.86
Definition: Using evidence to construct and evaluate claims, fostering critical thinking and perspective-taking.

Grade 3-6 Example: 6th graders hold a "Socratic Seminar" debating whether a historical figure was a hero or a villain, using specific quotes from their textbook to support their claim.
Concept Mapping d = 0.64
Definition: Visualizing how ideas are linked using lines and arrows to show cause-and-effect or hierarchy.

Grade 3-6 Example: Students create a web showing how "Climate," "Natural Resources," and "Jobs" were all connected in the development of the 13 Colonies.
Phase 3: Transfer Learning

This phase is about taking what was learned in one context and applying it to a completely new situation or problem.

SOLO Level: Extended Abstract (Click to view)
Extended Abstract: The student generalizes beyond the current subject. They can create new hypotheses or make connections to other domains (e.g., using planetary orbit concepts to explain how electrons move in an atom).
Problem-Solving Teaching d = 0.88
Definition: Presenting students with an unfamiliar, complex problem that requires them to pull from multiple areas of knowledge.

Grade 3-6 Example: Students are told that a local park is flooding. They must use what they know about the water cycle, soil types, and topography to design a drainage solution.
Transfer Strategies d = 0.75
Definition: Explicitly teaching students how to look for similarities between a "new" problem and an "old" one they've already solved.

Grade 3-6 Example: A teacher asks students to explain how the "Persuasion Techniques" they learned in English class are being used in a cereal commercial they saw on TV.
