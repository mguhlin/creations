Phase 1: Surface Learning

Acquire foundational knowledge and labels. Focus on the building blocks of the subject.

SOLO Levels: Uni-structural & Multi-structural (Click to view)
Uni-structural: Focusing on one relevant aspect (e.g., identifying a single character).
Multi-structural: Identifying several relevant aspects independently (e.g., listing character traits without connecting them to plot).
Jigsaw Method d = 0.92
Definition: Students master a subtopic in expert groups and teach it to their home group.

Example: In a 4th-grade geography lesson, each group learns about one specific landform (plateaus, valleys, etc.) to share with the class.
Spaced Practice d = 0.62
Definition: Distributing learning over time rather than cramming into one session.

Example: 3rd graders practice math facts for 5 minutes daily instead of one 25-minute block.
Phase 2: Deep Learning

Connecting ideas to build conceptual understanding and seeing how the "parts" relate to each other.

SOLO Level: Relational (Click to view)
Relational: Integrating several parts into a coherent whole. Understanding the "why" and "how" behind relationships.
Constructivist Teaching d = 0.90
Definition: A learner-centered approach where students actively explore ideas to construction personal meaning.

Example: 5th graders use a circuit kit to discover which materials are conductors or insulators through trial and error rather than being told.
Feedback (Timing) d = 0.89
Definition: Providing insights at the most effective moment—delayed feedback often promotes deeper reflection for complex tasks.

Example: A 6th-grade teacher provides feedback on an essay draft 24 hours later, allowing students time to re-read their own work with fresh eyes first.
Elaboration and Organization d = 0.72
Definition: Explaining ideas in detail, creating analogies, and arranging information logically/hierarchically.

Example: 4th graders use a Venn Diagram to compare and contrast two different ecosystems (Tundra vs. Rainforest) to understand common survival traits.
Phase 3: Transfer Learning

Applying knowledge flexibly to new, unfamiliar situations and creative problem-solving.

SOLO Level: Extended Abstract (Click to view)
Extended Abstract: Generalizing beyond the subject. Creating new hypotheses or applying concepts to different domains.
Self-Reported Grades d = 0.96
Definition: Students predict their own performance against rubrics to foster self-regulation and ownership.

Example: 5th graders review a rubric for their science project and "score" themselves before submitting, justifying why they believe they met the criteria.
Math Problem Solving d = 0.88
Definition: Using mathematical concepts to solve novel, ill-structured problems in real-world contexts.

Example: 6th graders are given a budget and a grocery flyer; they must plan a balanced meal for a family of four, applying decimals and percentages to a real scenario.
Virtual Reality (Science) d = 0.80
Definition: Using immersive simulations to apply scientific theories in safe, high-stakes, or impossible environments.

Example: 5th graders "visit" the International Space Station via VR to apply what they learned about gravity and orbit by simulating how an object moves in a vacuum.
