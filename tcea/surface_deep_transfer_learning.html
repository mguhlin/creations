Phase 1
Surface learning
Build foundational knowledge and vocabulary. Students acquire essential facts and skills before deeper connections are possible.
SOLO level
Uni-structural → Multi-structural
Students identify single ideas, then collect multiple disconnected pieces of knowledge.
High-impact strategies
Spaced practice Revisit content across distributed time intervals to strengthen long-term retention d = 0.62
Retrieval practice Low-stakes quizzing and recall activities that consolidate memory more than re-reading d = 0.49
Direct instruction Explicit teacher-led explanation with modeling, checking for understanding, and corrective feedback d = 0.56
Vocabulary instruction Systematic word study tied to disciplinary concepts, with multiple exposures across contexts d = 0.65
Worked examples Step-by-step solutions that reduce cognitive load while students build schema d = 0.57
Summarization Students distill key ideas from text or instruction into their own words d = 0.45
Mnemonics Memory devices that create retrieval cues for facts, sequences, and terminology d = 0.65
Note-taking Structured recording of key ideas that promotes active processing during input d = 0.50

Phase 2
Deep learning
Connect ideas and build conceptual understanding. Students move from knowing facts in isolation to seeing how they relate.
SOLO level
Relational
Students integrate multiple ideas into a coherent whole, understanding how parts relate to each other and to the whole.
High-impact strategies
Classroom discussion Structured dialogue where students share, challenge, and refine thinking with peers and teacher d = 0.82
Reciprocal teaching Students rotate roles — summarizer, questioner, clarifier, predictor — to build comprehension d = 0.74
Metacognitive strategies Students monitor, plan, and regulate their own understanding and thinking processes d = 0.60
Concept mapping Visual diagrams that make relationships between ideas explicit and spatially organized d = 0.64
Peer tutoring Students teaching students, which deepens understanding for both tutor and learner d = 0.55
Argumentation Structured use of evidence to construct, defend, and evaluate competing claims d = 0.86
Jigsaw Expert groups develop deep knowledge of one piece, then teach it to a mixed group d = 0.92
Questioning Higher-order questions that prompt students to analyze, evaluate, and justify their thinking d = 0.49

Phase 3
Transfer learning
Apply knowledge to new, unfamiliar situations. Students demonstrate that understanding is flexible and generative, not just recalled.
SOLO level
Extended abstract
Students generalize beyond taught content, create new hypotheses, make connections across domains, and generate novel solutions.
High-impact strategies
Problem-solving teaching Novel, ill-structured problems that require flexible application of knowledge across contexts d = 0.88
Service learning Real-world community tasks that demand authentic application of academic content d = 0.58
Self-regulation strategies Students set goals, monitor progress, and adapt strategies independently across new tasks d = 0.52
Deliberate practice Focused, goal-directed repetition with feedback to build automaticity that frees cognitive resources d = 0.79
Transfer strategies Explicit prompting for students to connect learning across contexts, subjects, and time periods d = 0.75
Project-based learning Extended, real-world inquiry tasks requiring students to synthesize and apply knowledge to produce an artifact d = 0.43
