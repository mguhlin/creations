The C.A.R.E. Framework

C.A.R.E. helps librarians move from individual experimentation to shared, ethical practice supported by clear guardrails. C.A.R.E. guides values. VIVA guides thinking. Use both.

Critical Awareness
Acknowledge AI's flaws and biases
  • Questions AI-generated content rather than accepting it
  • Identifies cultural and demographic bias in AI outputs
  • Checks AI book recommendations for collection gaps
  • Treats AI as a starting point, never a final authority
  • Teaches students source evaluation skills for AI
Applied Purpose
Use AI to augment, not replace, judgment
  • Defines a clear, constructive goal before using any AI tool
  • Focuses AI on tasks that free time for student engagement
  • Pairs AI use with a professional task requiring judgment
  • Avoids using AI to bypass critical curatorial decisions
  • Asks: "What good am I trying to achieve?"
Responsible Practice
Use AI safely, transparently, with integrity
  • Never inputs student PIIPersonally Identifiable Information (PII) — Any data that can identify a specific individual, such as names, addresses, student ID numbers, or biometric data. Under FERPA and state privacy laws, student PII requires strict handling and consent protocols. into any AI tool without a DPAData Processing Agreement (DPA) — A legally binding contract between a data controller (the school/district) and a data processor (the vendor) that specifies how student data will be handled, stored, protected, and deleted. Required for FERPA compliance when sharing student data with third parties.
  • Labels all AI-generated library content clearly
  • Supports school-wide AI policy development
  • Distinguishes enterprise AIEnterprise AI — AI tools purchased or licensed by the school or district under a formal agreement that includes data privacy protections, a DPA, and administrative controls. Contrast with free consumer AI tools (ChatGPT free tier, Google Gemini, etc.) that may use input data for model training. from free/consumer AI tools
  • Models the academic integrity standards taught to students
Evaluative Outcome
Reflect on impact and continuously improve
  • Regularly assesses whether AI tools achieve their purpose
  • Monitors for equity disparities in AI-assisted services
  • Gathers feedback from students, teachers, and families
  • Documents lessons learned and shares across the school
  • Adjusts policies based on evidence and reflection

The VIVA Discussion Method

Use VIVA to structure your thinking on every scenario. Each step builds toward a concrete guardrail your library can act on.

V
Verify — What do we know?
  • What is actually happening in this scenario?
  • Who is deciding, and what is the decision being made?
  • What facts do you know for certain versus assume?
I
Illustrate — What's the context?
  • What could go wrong, even unintentionally?
  • Who might be affected — students, families, staff?
  • Which community members are most at risk here?
V
Validate — Is it sound?
  • Which C.A.R.E. principle is most at risk?
  • Why does it matter in the school library context?
  • What professional value is being tested?
A
Apply — What will we do?
  • What one guardrail reduces risk next time?
  • What would you change first?
  • Who needs to be at the table to make this decision?

Closing Reflection — From Scenarios to Guardrails

Humans remain accountable for the outputs, decisions, and actions. End every session with a concrete commitment.

Discuss as a Group

  • What assumption did these scenarios surface?
  • Where is your highest-risk AI use today?
  • What is one guardrail you will implement first?
  • Where should it live: policy, checklist, training, or review?

If You Want a Starting Point

  • Create a no-upload list for student PIIPersonally Identifiable Information (PII) — Any data that can identify a specific individual, such as names, addresses, student ID numbers, or biometric data. and sensitive content
  • Require source verification for all AI-generated resource lists
  • Add a fairness check to any AI-generated collection recommendations
  • Set a quarterly review cycle for your AI guardrails and policies

Your "We Will / We Will Not" Statement

  • Write one "We will..." commitment your library can own
  • Write one "We will not..." boundary you can enforce
  • Name an owner and a due date for each
  • Share it at the next faculty or department meeting
