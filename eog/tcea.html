Conduct & Key Policies

All employees are expected to uphold TCEA's professional standards. Below are the key conduct policies you should understand on Day 1. A full copy of the Employee Handbook is provided by HR — read it thoroughly and sign the acknowledgment form.

Policy

Anti-Harassment

TCEA has zero tolerance for harassment based on race, gender, religion, national origin, disability, or any protected class. Report immediately to your supervisor or the Executive Director. All reports are investigated promptly.

Zero Tolerance

Violence & Weapons

No weapons or firearms are permitted on TCEA property, including parking areas and in personal vehicles on TCEA grounds. Any threat or act of violence is grounds for immediate termination.

Drug-Free

Drug-Free Workplace

TCEA maintains a drug-free workplace policy. Employees may not possess, use, or distribute illegal substances on any TCEA property or at any TCEA-related activity. Violations result in immediate termination.

Dress Code

Appearance

TCEA's official dress code is casual (tasteful shirts/tops, nice jeans, sneakers). Upgrade to business casual when the Board is present or TCEA is hosting an event. Business professional required if you are presenting at a major event.

Communications

Communication Systems

All email, phone, and internet systems are TCEA property. You have no expectation of privacy. Do not use TCEA systems for personal gain or to promote political/religious causes. All communications are subject to monitoring.

Separation

At-Will Employment

TCEA is an at-will employer. Either party may terminate employment at any time. If you resign, provide at least 2 weeks' written notice to be eligible for accrued vacation payout and rehire consideration.

Board/Staff Communication Rule: Copy the Executive Director on all email correspondence with board members. Respond to board emails within one business day. Set an out-of-office reply if you will be unavailable for more than one day.

Responsible AI Usage Policy

As an edtech association, TCEA encourages staff to use AI tools responsibly. This policy governs how generative and interactive AI may be used in your work. All employees are expected to understand and follow these guidelines.

Transparency

Disclose AI Use

If AI is responsible for 50% or more of any content you create, you must publicly disclose it (e.g., "This document was created using [AI tool] and edited for clarity by a TCEA staff member").

Accountability

Humans Stay Responsible

TCEA will never publish content written entirely by AI without human review. AI is an assistant — not a replacement for good judgment. You are accountable for every piece of content you publish.

Privacy

Protect Member Data

Never share private member information or confidential TCEA business/financial data with AI language models. This is a strict privacy requirement, not a guideline.

Prohibited

No Impersonation

Do not use AI to impersonate any person without their explicit permission. Do not use AI to create fully AI-generated images or designs for items TCEA sells (branding, merchandise, courses, ebooks).

Bias & Ethics

Review for Bias

All AI-assisted content must be reviewed for bias, inaccuracies, and alignment with TCEA's inclusive values before publication. AI can perpetuate bias — human review is not optional.

Copyright

Copyright Awareness

AI-generated images cannot currently be copyrighted. Do not use fully AI-generated assets in products TCEA sells or brands. Review copyright implications for any AI-generated content before publication.

Acceptance: By using AI in your work at TCEA, you agree to this policy. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action including termination. Questions? Ask the Executive Director.

Telecommuting Policy

Telecommuting is a privilege, not an entitlement. It is available to eligible employees who have maintained satisfactory performance. You must be in the office on all Monday staff meetings regardless of your telecommute schedule.

Eligibility

When You Can Start

After 6 months: eligible for 1 remote day/week. After 9 months: eligible for 2 days. After 1 year: eligible for up to 3 days. All telecommute agreements are reviewed annually by the Executive Director.

Required In-Office

Monday Is Always In-Office

One in-office day each week must be Monday (staff meetings). The Executive Director may designate other mandatory in-office days throughout the year (e.g., convention week, board meeting days, event opening days).

Requirements

What You Need

Fast internet access at your remote location is required. TCEA generally does not pay for home internet or equipment. If you were issued a TCEA laptop, you may use it for TCEA work only at your remote location.

Safety

Remote Work Safety

You are responsible for maintaining a safe remote work environment. Injuries while performing TCEA work at your remote location are covered by workers' compensation. Report any injury to the Executive Director immediately.

Important: Telecommuting agreements may be revised or discontinued at any time at TCEA's discretion. The arrangement does not create a contract of employment. Failure to comply with the telecommuting policy results in immediate termination of the arrangement.
