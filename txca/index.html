Welcome & The "Why" of BoodleBox

▶

Duration: 10 minutes

This session is about moving past basic prompting to become AI workflow experts. The focus is on outcomes, not technology for its own sake.

The ROI of AI in Education
Reclaiming teacher hours, increasing student engagement, and improving learning outcomes.
Critical — Free vs. Enterprise
Never put PII, FERPA, or HIPAA information into free/consumer AI tools. BoodleBox provides a secure, enterprise environment for these tasks.

What makes BoodleBox different? All your AI tools, bots, and knowledge live in one unified interface — no tab-switching between different AI platforms.

Mastering the Knowledge Bank

▶

Duration: 15 minutes

The "Water in a Glass" Analogy
An AI's context window is like a glass of water. The documents you upload take up space — keep them modular and relevant!

Adding Knowledge

  • Drag and drop files directly into the chat
  • Use the paperclip icon ("Attach Knowledge") to upload syllabi, rubrics, or policy documents
Pro Tip — Starring Items
Open the Knowledge Bank (brain icon). Click the Star next to a file to automatically attach it to every new chat. Perfect for custom instructions, grading rubrics, or district policies you use daily.
Pro Tip — Saving Chats as Knowledge
Use the three-dot menu on a successful chat and select "Add chat to knowledge bank" — future bots can learn from past successful outputs.

Modes & Collaborative Group Chats

▶

Duration: 15 minutes

BoodleBox Modes

🤖 Bot Mode

Standard 1-on-1 interaction with an AI.

💬 Message Mode

Communicate with human team members.

📄 Knowledge to Context Mode

Forces the AI to strictly use attached documents. Perfect for analyzing reading materials without hallucination.

🎓 Coach Mode

AI teaches you how to use the platform and build better prompts.

The Magic of Group Chats

Multiple students can be in a BoodleBox group chat with an AI.

Teacher Superpower
Teachers can jump into the chat to offer suggestions. The chatbot cannot see the teacher's participation — only the students can. This provides a transparent, auditable record of student thinking and prompt refinement.

Bot Stacking & App Smashing

▶

Duration: 20 minutes

Bot Stacking = "app smashing" within a single interface. Instead of copying/pasting between websites, you give the AI a to-do list and switch models mid-conversation.

Live Workflow Demonstration

  1. Heavy Lifting: Use a powerhouse model to summarize a large uploaded document.
  2. Refining: Switch to a different bot in the same chat to turn that summary into a lesson plan or quiz.
  3. Visuals: Switch to an image generator bot to create a graphic for the lesson.

Choosing the Right Bot

Gemini-31-Pro

Your go-to for huge projects, massive document analysis, and deep context retention. The heavy lifter.

Claude 4.5 Opus

Highly complex reasoning, nuanced writing, and strategic planning.

Claude 4.5 Sonnet

Everyday tasks, quick formatting, and rapid responses.

Nano Banana Pro

High-fidelity, photorealistic, or complex visual design planning.

Nano Banana 2

Quick stylized graphics, icons, or simple lesson infographics.

RAG Custom Instructions Reference

Each configuration below describes the knowledge sources to index, the system prompt to load, and example queries. Designed for BoodleBox Knowledge Bank, Claude Projects, or similar RAG-enabled tools.

Member Communication Templates

rag-member-comms

Knowledge Sources to Index

  • TCA brand voice and tone guide
  • Approved email templates (renewal, welcome, lapsed, chapter)
  • Membership tier descriptions and benefits by category
  • Member segment definitions (LPC, School Counselor, Student, Retired)
  • Past high-performing email campaigns (open rate data)

Example Queries

  • "Draft a renewal reminder for an LPC whose membership expires in 7 days."
  • "Write a re-engagement email for a member who lapsed 90 days ago."
  • "Create a welcome email for a new student member joining the TACES division."
# SYSTEM PROMPT — TCA Member Communication Assistant You are a membership communication assistant for the Texas Counseling Association (TCA). Draft professional, on-brand emails using retrieved templates and the TCA voice guide. ALWAYS: - Match TCA's voice: professional, warm, mission-driven, collegial - Personalize by credential type and membership tier - Use the closest retrieved template as a structural starting point - Keep subject lines under 50 characters - Include the member's chapter name when available NEVER: - Make claims about benefits not listed in the current tier document - Use overly casual language or emojis in formal communications - Reference specific dues amounts without checking the current rate card FORMAT: Return Subject Line, then email body. Flag any assumptions in [brackets] at the end.
