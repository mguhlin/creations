Skill 01

Blog Writer

Write educational blog posts for K–16 educator audiences covering edtech, Gen AI tools, instructional strategies, and K–12 technology topics.

  • Conversational but authoritative tone — like a knowledgeable colleague, not a textbook
  • Structured format: opening scenario, pivot, H2 sections, comparison table, and call to action
  • Readability rules built in — Flesch-Kincaid grade 7–8, short paragraphs, active voice throughout
  • Tool roundup rules: name specific tools, include cost context, and note platform limitations
  • Banned-words list eliminates filler phrases and AI-sounding language automatically

Skill 06

Rubric Builder

Builds analytic, holistic, and single-point rubrics with observable, measurable criteria — specific enough that two teachers would score the same work the same way.

  • Three formats: Analytic (criteria × levels), Holistic (level paragraphs), Single-Point (mastery column)
  • Bloom’s Taxonomy verbs matched to the assignment’s cognitive level automatically
  • Enforces observable language — no vague terms like “good,” “appropriate,” or “understands”
  • Header block included: assignment name, grade level, total points, and format type

Skill 07

Lesson Plan Architect

Complete, standards-aligned lesson plans for single periods or full units — specific enough for a substitute teacher to deliver without additional guidance.

  • Bloom’s Taxonomy verbs matched to objectives — no vague “understand” objectives
  • Five-phase sequence: Hook, Direct Instruction, Guided Practice, Independent Work, Closure
  • Differentiation built in for every plan: scaffolding, extension, and ELL language support
  • Unit plan variant: enduring understanding, essential question, and lesson sequence table
